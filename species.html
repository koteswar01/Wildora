SPECIES

Mugger Crocodile

The mugger crocodile, or marsh crocodile, is a broad-snouted reptile native to South Asia. It thrives in freshwater habitats, digs burrows, uses tools, and protects its young. This species, vulnerable due to habitat loss and human conflict, plays a vital role in wetland ecosystems across India, Nepal, and Sri Lanka.

Gharial

The gharial (Gavialis gangeticus) is a critically endangered, fish-eating crocodilian with a long, narrow snout and over 100 sharp teeth. Native to northern India and Nepal, it inhabits deep, fast-flowing rivers. Males develop a bulbous nasal growth called a “ghara,” and the species rarely leaves water except to bask or nest.

Saltwater Crocodile

The saltwater crocodile is the world’s largest reptile, found from India to Australia in coastal and brackish waters. Known for aggressive ambush hunting, it feeds on diverse prey. Males can exceed 6 meters. It nests in warm months, and though listed as Least Concern, it faces habitat and poaching threats.

King Cobra

The king cobra (Ophiophagus hannah) is the world’s longest venomous snake, reaching up to 5.6 meters. Native to South and Southeast Asia, it preys mainly on other snakes. Known for its hood display and potent neurotoxic venom, it builds nests and fiercely guards its eggs. It’s listed as Vulnerable.

Russell's Viper

The Russell’s viper (Daboia russelii) is a highly venomous snake found across South Asia. Recognized by its chain-like pattern, it’s responsible for many snakebite deaths in India. It prefers open grasslands and farmlands, feeds on rodents, and delivers a painful, hemotoxic bite. It’s part of India’s “Big Four” deadly snakes.

Common Krait

The common krait (Bungarus caeruleus) is a highly venomous, nocturnal snake found across South Asia. Glossy black with white bands, it hides in burrows by day. Its neurotoxic venom causes paralysis, often without pain, making bites deceptively dangerous. It’s one of India’s “Big Four” snakes responsible for most fatal bites.

Indian Rock Python

The Indian rock python (Python molurus) is a large, nonvenomous constrictor found across South Asia. It inhabits forests, grasslands, and wetlands. Typically 2.5–3 meters long, it ambushes prey like rodents and birds. Solitary and slow-moving, it’s an excellent swimmer and climber. It’s listed as Near Threatened due to habitat loss.

Anaconda

The anaconda, especially the green anaconda (Eunectes murinus), is one of the world’s largest snakes by weight. Native to South America’s swamps and rivers, it’s a powerful constrictor. Ambush predator by nature, it preys on fish, birds, and mammals. Despite its size, it’s elusive and rarely poses a threat to humans.

Green Iguana

The green iguana (Iguana iguana) is a large, herbivorous lizard native to Central and South America. Arboreal and excellent swimmers, they thrive in tropical forests near water. Recognizable by their spiny crests and dewlaps, they bask in sunlight and feed on leaves. Despite popularity as pets, they require specialized care.

Monitor Lizard

The monitor lizard is a large, carnivorous reptile found across Africa, Asia, and Oceania. With strong limbs, sharp claws, and long tails, it thrives in diverse habitats. Some species, like the Komodo dragon, grow over 3 meters. Intelligent and adaptable, monitors are skilled hunters, feeding on insects, birds, and small mammals.

Indian Star Tortoise

The Indian star tortoise (Geochelone elegans) is a small, herbivorous reptile native to India, Sri Lanka, and Pakistan. Named for its star-like shell patterns, it inhabits dry forests and scrublands. Popular in the pet trade, it’s listed as Vulnerable due to habitat loss and illegal collection despite international protection.

Sea Turtle

Sea turtles are marine reptiles with streamlined shells and flipper-like limbs, adapted for life in oceans. Seven species exist, including green and leatherback turtles. They migrate vast distances, nest on beaches, and face threats like habitat loss, pollution, and fishing nets. Most are endangered, making conservation efforts critically important.

Reef Shark

Sharks are ancient, cartilaginous fish with streamlined bodies, multiple gill slits, and rows of replaceable teeth. Found in oceans worldwide, they range from tiny lanternsharks to massive whale sharks. Some are apex predators, while others filter-feed. Despite fearsome reputations, most pose little threat to humans. Many species face overfishing threats.

Freshwater Fish

Freshwater fish live in rivers, lakes, and wetlands with low salt levels. They include species like catfish, trout, carp, and tilapia. Adapted to diverse habitats, they regulate salt balance through specialized gills and kidneys. Many face threats from pollution, habitat loss, and overfishing, making conservation efforts increasingly important for biodiversity.
